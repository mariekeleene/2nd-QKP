Title: Concrete Lover

Year: 2025

Medium: text/ film/ photo

Showed at: Jelsa Art Biennial 2025/ Concrete Tenderness

text with the work: And so we keep meeting, always in waiting, never in having to.
I see you everywhere,
on the corner of the street where I get my morning coffee,
between the cars driving by,
in front of the beach and under the vegetable stall.
you see me too, I'm sure.
but the step from seeing to becoming is a big one.
so we stay here: watching, silent, getting close, and then disappearing again.
in the distance dwells the uncertainty;
in the uncertainty reigns the eternal hope that sooner or later do you in.
how can something be everywhere,
and yet remain invisible?
i laugh, for it is the same story,
the repetition catching up,
what can i do but laugh.
“there is a promise in that look,
a faith that almost preaches:
we are going to do it different.
formless we will be,
no mold that can catch us.
nothing is fixed here,
there are no loopholes,
for who thinks of laws for this?
no one will understand but us
armed as you lie there like that,
i kiss your gray skin softly.
in all things resistant to my ballast,
even when you are old and with a crack here and there.
you lift me from my heaviness,
always whispering what you dreamed for us:
to be liquid, to become hard.

And that's what you became,
harder through time,
stronger under pressure.

Let me love you, love you hard: relentlessly, grinding, and cold.

here's nothing here but concrete to bump into,
nothing but dust to swallow.
You are trapped in a mold getting harder and harder every minute,
forgetting by yourself how it was:

to be liquid
