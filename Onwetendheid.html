Free Embroidery — 2024

15 × 15 cm
Made by Maria Virginia de Almeida Aguiar

This hand‑embroidered work was created in 2024 and draws inspiration from the practice of Dutch artist Marieke Leene, particularly her installation presented at the ArtEZ Finals 2022 in Arnhem. Leene’s work places her own body at the center of a sharp yet humorous reflection on environmental damage, consumer culture, and their entanglement with global political dynamics.

Through embroidery, Maria Virginia translates this critical perspective into textile form, connecting contemporary artistic discourse with traditional handcraft. The piece reflects on nature, culture, and social responsibility while honoring embroidery as a daily, living practice passed down through generations of women.

Her practice is rooted in Brazilian embroidery traditions—especially from Minas Gerais and the Northeast—as well as Indigenous artisanship. It also converses with textile cultures from Portugal, Peru, Colombia, Mexico, and historical European embroidery, weaving together ancestral knowledge and contemporary art.
